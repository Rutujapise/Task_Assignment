Personal info

Sunita Williams was born on September 19, 1965, in Ohio, USA. She comes from an Indian-Slovenian family; her father, Dr. Deepak Pandya,is of Indian origin, and her mother, Ursuline Bonnie Pandya, has Slovenian roots. She is married to Michael J. Williams, a former helicopter pilot. Her hobbies include running, swimming, biking, surfing, and participating in triathlons. She also has a deep connection to her heritage and carried the Bhagavad Gita to space.

Sunita Williams' Major Achievements

Sunita Williams is a distinguished NASA astronaut who has spent 608 days in space, making her one of the most experienced astronauts. She holds the record for nine spacewalks, totaling hours and 6 minutes, the highest for a woman at the time. In 2007, she became the first person to run a marathon in space, completing the Boston Marathon aboard the ISS. She also served as the commander of the International Space Station (ISS) during Expedition 33. In 2024, she piloted Boeing’s first crewed Starliner mission to the ISS, marking a historic milestone. Her exceptional contributions to space exploration have earned her prestigious honors, including the NASA Spaceflight Medal, Legion of Merit, and India’s Padma Bhushan. Beyond space, she actively promotes STEM education and space awareness. Williams has also represented her Indian and Slovenian heritage, carrying religious texts and national symbols to space. In recognition of her legacy, a school in Needham, Massachusetts, has been named after her.
